Introduction to Cognitive Science

Cognitive science is an interdisciplinary study of mind and intelligence lying at the intersection of artificial intelligence, psychology, neurobiology, education, linguistics, anthropology, and philosophy. In this course, students are introduced to the basic concepts, hypotheses, models, methods, issues, and debates in cognitive science.

Human-Computer Interaction

Human–Computer Interaction is an interdisciplinary field that studies how people interact with technology and how systems can be designed to better support human needs. Drawing from computer science, psychology, design, and social sciences, the field focuses on usability, user experience, and the development of intuitive interfaces. In this course, students are introduced to the core principles, design methods, evaluation techniques, and research approaches used to create effective and human-centered computing systems.

Data Analytics and Security

Data Analytics and Security is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on extracting insights from large datasets while ensuring that information systems remain secure and trustworthy. Drawing from computer science, statistics, and cybersecurity, the field explores techniques for analyzing data, identifying patterns, and protecting sensitive information from misuse or attack. In this course, students are introduced to the fundamental concepts, analytical methods, and security principles used to manage, analyze, and safeguard data in modern computing environments.

Digital Health Equity

Digital Health Equity is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on ensuring that digital health technologies are accessible, inclusive, and beneficial for diverse populations. Drawing from public health, computer science, policy, and social sciences, the field examines how technology can improve healthcare access while addressing disparities caused by socioeconomic, geographic, and technological barriers. In this course, students are introduced to the key concepts, frameworks, and design considerations used to develop digital health solutions that promote fairness, accessibility, and improved health outcomes.
