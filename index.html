Introduction to Cognitive Science

Cognitive science is an interdisciplinary study of mind and intelligence lying at the intersection of artificial intelligence, psychology, neurobiology, education, linguistics, anthropology, and philosophy. In this course, students are introduced to the basic concepts, hypotheses, models, methods, issues, and debates in cognitive science.

Human-Computer Interaction

Human–Computer Interaction is an interdisciplinary field that studies how people interact with technology and how systems can be designed to better support human needs. Drawing from computer science, psychology, design, and social sciences, the field focuses on usability, user experience, and the development of intuitive interfaces. In this course, students are introduced to the core principles, design methods, evaluation techniques, and research approaches used to create effective and human-centered computing systems.
